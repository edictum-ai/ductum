# What an AI Factory Needs — Reference Architecture & Ductum Gap-Map

*Derived 2026-06-16 from 7 independent lenses (work-lifecycle, control/data-plane, governance, Day-2 reliability, operator interface, executor/harness, mature-systems analogies), then gap-mapped against `ductum/inventory/`.*

**How to read this.** The left side is the reference — the parts an autonomous agent-work factory must have, regardless of product. The **Ductum status** column maps each part to the inventory: **HAVE** (solid), **PARTIAL** (exists, incomplete), **WEAK** (exists but fragile / flagged REDESIGN), **MISSING** (no real implementation).

## Headline

An AI agent-work factory needs three things layered together: a durable, externalized work-state model that survives crashes; an in-process enforcement chokepoint that gates every tool call and stage advance against evidence; and a control loop that dispatches heterogeneous executors, captures attestable evidence, routes failures, and keeps a human at the approval boundary. Ductum has the enforcement core and the lifecycle spine solid, but its distributed-systems safety layer (idempotency, leasing/fencing, checkpointed recovery) and its execution confinement (scoped secrets, real sandbox isolation) are the load-bearing gaps.

## What an AI factory needs

A factory that runs autonomous agents at scale is a distributed control system, not an agent runner. The recurring lesson across all seven lenses is that the hard parts are not "make the agent edit code" — they are everything around it: a single authoritative record of what every unit of work is doing, a structural point where a forbidden action can actually be stopped, and a recovery story that survives a crash without re-running or double-merging. The layers below are ordered roughly by the path a unit of work takes through the system.

### Intake & Planning
Work enters from heterogeneous sources and must be normalized into one canonical record, bound to a target, and decomposed into a dependency graph of dispatchable tasks. This layer also distinguishes "implementing from scratch" from narrower "fixing" remediation (C7), because they carry different allowed-action and evidence profiles. Without it, malformed work leaks deep into dispatch and there is no parallelism or partial progress.

### Work Model & State
The spine of the whole factory: a single authoritative enum of lifecycle states with legal transitions, owned by exactly one transition engine, persisted in durable externalized storage — never agent memory, never prompt text. Agents request advancement (gate_check); the engine adjudicates. This is what makes "verified" and "approved" mean something and what makes crash recovery possible at all.

### Scheduling & Dispatch
Decides which ready task runs on which executor, when, under what concurrency and budget caps. It resolves readiness from the graph, freezes a config snapshot at dispatch so the rules can't change mid-run, applies backpressure so bursts don't blow rate limits, and — critically in a distributed setting — uses leases with fencing tokens and idempotency keys so a presumed-dead worker can't resume and corrupt state, and retries collapse to a single effect.

### Execution & Workers
A sealed, versioned job bundle is the one contract every executor consumes identically, behind a harness/adapter abstraction with capability negotiation, so a local Claude SDK worker and a remote container worker are interchangeable backends. This layer owns sandbox isolation, the authoritative session-to-run binding, scoped secret brokering, lifecycle control (timeout/cancel/retry), and liveness/heartbeat supervision.

### Governance & Enforcement
The wedge. An in-process tool-call interceptor routes every action to a local, fail-closed policy engine before the side effect happens — structural, not advisory. Ordered process gates (read-before-edit, verify-before-push, no-done-before-CI), evidence-gated progression, least-privilege capability grants, protected-branch limits, and egress scanning for secrets all live here. Reset authority belongs to the control plane, not the agent.

### Evidence & Audit
Durable, content-addressed, immutable evidence is the substance every gate adjudicates and the deliverable an auditor inspects. A tamper-evident append-only event log is both the audit spine and the integration bus. Without an artifact store, gates trust agent self-report and the governance claim is unfalsifiable.

### Verification & Quality Gates
Runs the target's declared verify commands in-sandbox, then coordinates CI and review as two parallel latches that must both resolve before the merge gate (C6). Independent watchers ingest out-of-band signals (CI webhooks) keyed by commit SHA as first-class governed events. This layer enforces that verification happened — an honest boundary, not a correctness guarantee.

### Integration & Approval
The human-in-the-loop boundary pauses risky transitions and resumes only on an explicit, audited operator decision. The merge orchestrator then serializes merges, enforces branch discipline, and mediates the irreversible push/merge through an exactly-once boundary so a crash mid-merge neither loses nor doubles the effect.

### Reliability & Recovery
Day-2 survival: checkpoint-and-resume instead of retry-from-scratch, a reconciler that converges externalized state against live runtime reality after a crash, stall detection with control-plane-owned reset, poison-task quarantine, graceful drain on deploy, and reaping of orphaned worktrees/sandboxes/branches. This is the most-forgotten layer and the one that decides whether the factory survives its first restart.

### Cost & Resource
A transactional budget ledger meters spend per attempt/task/spec, attributes it to the right mode (implementing vs fixing), and enforces hard stops that block dispatch or halt a runaway before the bill arrives.

### Operator Surfaces
A unified run-state view, a needs-you inbox, approval/intervention controls, a per-run evidence timeline, cost visibility, and a portable decision bundle — all available identically through CLI and UI, plus honest readiness/doctor status. Supervision does not scale past one or two runs without these.

### Biggest gaps for Ductum

Ductum's enforcement core (in-process authorize_tool, gate_check, ordered gates, evidence kinds, session-to-run binding, C1/C3/C4/C5 discipline) and its lifecycle spine (RunStateMachine, DAG evaluator, dispatch/spawn, post-completion router, CI/review latch, approval wedge, repair/readiness) are genuinely solid — this is where 64% KEEP comes from. The load-bearing gaps cluster in two places. First, **execution confinement**: dispatch spreads the full host `process.env` (incl. `ANTHROPIC_API_KEY`) into every agent while the encrypted FactorySecret store is wired only to notifications — four high-risk REDESIGN rows — and the sandbox is laptop-bound (host/worktree only) with a cost scanner that reads the operator's whole home tree and can silently record $0. Second, **distributed-systems safety**: there is no idempotency-key / exactly-once boundary on side effects (the evidence INSERT itself is non-idempotent), no leasing-with-fencing-tokens (live state lives in an in-memory `activeSessions` Map), no checkpoint/resume (crash retry rebuilds the whole task from `understand`, losing all progress), no content-addressed caching/dedup, and no schema-version compatibility gate. Notification fan-out is effectively Telegram-only against an unrealized D055 multi-channel vision, and the portable signed decision bundle exists only as transcript surfaces. These gaps are exactly the ones that bite when the factory moves off one laptop toward the SaaS future.
